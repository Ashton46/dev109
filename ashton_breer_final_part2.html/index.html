Web Development Final Reflection

  • What was your previous knowledge of HTML/CSS/Javascript?

    Before this class, I had no knowledge with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I haven't taken any coding classes either. At the beggining of this class it was very overwhelming at the beginning of the class as we were moving through topics very quickly. Once I got a good base understanding, everything was more manageable. .

  • Did you use a template or did you build your portfolio from scratch and why?

    I did use the template that was given to us for the midterm. I just carried over the template from teh midterm to my final project. The template was very useful as it had a set consistent structure throughout the website. I felt that having a template was very valuable to make the website look professional and pass the usability and accessibility test.

  • What was your most challenging part of your portfolio (ie, the template, artifacts, CSS, etc.)?

    The most challenging part of my portfolio was the CSS. Ensuring that the layout looked right was very important. Making sure the design was responsive and user-friendly required a lot of attention to detail and iterative testing.

  • How did this class change your view on Web Development?

    This class changed my view on web development as I now understand the amount of time and effort that is put into creating a professional looking webiste. I learned that web development is not just about coding, but also about problem-solving and designing user-friendly interfaces.

  • What would you recommend to future students taking this class?

    I would recommend future students to start working on their projects early and to make sure to practice a lot because all the topics from different weeks build on each other. Practicing coding regularly and reviewing course materials consistently will make the learning process smoother.
